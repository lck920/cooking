## Ingredients
- 900 g skin-on pork belly
- 10 small shallots, thinly sliced
- 8 cloves garlic, minced
- 4 slices ginger
- 10 dried shiitake mushrooms
- 3 cups hot water
- 1 cup neutral oil
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 2 star anise
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp five-spice powder
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 2 tbsp rock sugar
- 1/4 cup Shaoxing wine
- 1/4 cup light soy sauce
- 3 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 6–8 hard-boiled eggs

## Steps
1. Soak the dried shiitake mushrooms in 3 cups of hot water for 30 minutes.
2. Slice the soaked mushrooms and save the mushroom soaking water.
3. Slice the shallots, garlic, and ginger.
4. Heat neutral oil in a pot and fry the shallots until golden brown.
5. Remove the fried shallots and set aside. Keep the shallot oil in the pot.
6. Add pork belly into the shallot oil and sauté until the water evaporates and the fat starts to sizzle.
7. Add garlic and ginger, then sauté until fragrant.
8. Add shiitake mushrooms, cinnamon stick, star anise, bay leaves, five-spice powder, white pepper, and rock sugar.
9. Add Shaoxing wine, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, fried shallots, and the mushroom soaking water.
10. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 45 minutes.
11. Optional: add hard-boiled eggs and simmer for another 30 minutes.
12. Serve hot with rice.

## Tips
1. Fry the shallots slowly so they become golden and crispy, not burnt.
2. Saving the mushroom water gives the sauce more flavour.
3. Simmer longer if you want the pork belly softer.
4. The braised eggs taste better after soaking in the sauce.
