## Ingredients
- 1 steak
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste

## Steps

1. Bring the steak to room temperature.
2. Pat the steak dry with kitchen paper.
3. Season with black pepper.
4. Add salt at least 20 minutes before cooking, or after cooking.
5. Place the steak into a cold non-stick pan. Do not add oil.
6. Turn the heat to high and cook the first side for 2 minutes.
7. Flip and cook the second side for 2 minutes.
8. Turn the heat to low.
9. For a thin steak, cook each side for another 1 minute.
10. For a thick steak, keep flipping every 1–2 minutes until cooked to your preferred doneness.
11. Remove the steak from the pan and rest for 5–6 minutes.
12. Slice and serve.

## Tips
1. Patting the steak dry helps it brown better.
2. Cold searing starts the steak in a cold pan and uses the steak’s own fat.
3. Resting the steak helps keep the juices inside.
4. Use a meat thermometer for the most accurate doneness.

## Doneness Guide
- Rare: 50–52°C
- Medium rare: 54–57°C
- Medium: 60–63°C
- Medium well: 65–68°C
- Well done: 70°C and above
