## Ingredients
- 4–5 chicken drumsticks
- 2 tbsp peanut oil
- 150ml Filipino soy sauce
- 20 black peppercorns
- 2 star anise
- 3 bay leaves
- 300ml water
- 1 head garlic, peeled and finely diced
- 100ml coconut vinegar
- 4 soft-boiled eggs
- Rice, to serve

## Steps
1. Heat peanut oil in a large frying pan or pot over medium-high heat.
2. Add the chicken drumsticks and sear until browned on all sides.
3. Add Filipino soy sauce, black peppercorns, star anise, bay leaves, and water.
4. Bring to a simmer, cover, and cook for 15 minutes over medium-low heat.
5. Add the diced garlic and cook for another 10 minutes.
6. Add the coconut vinegar and continue cooking uncovered.
7. Reduce the sauce by half. Do not cover the pan after adding vinegar so the acidity can cook off.
8. Add the soft-boiled eggs during the last 10 minutes.
9. Cook on low heat and turn the eggs gently so they get an even colour.
10. Serve hot with rice.

## Tips
1. Do not stir too much after adding vinegar at the beginning; let it cook first to reduce the sharp acidity.
2. Use low heat when adding eggs so they do not break easily.
3. The sauce should be savoury, slightly tangy, and rich.
4. Best served with warm white rice.
