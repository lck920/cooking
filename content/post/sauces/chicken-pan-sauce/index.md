## Options
- Fond: browned bits left in the pan after cooking chicken
- Aromatics: onion and/or garlic
- Liquid: water, stock, wine, beer, cider or coffee
- Fat: butter, cream or oil
- Finisher: herbs, sauces or lemon juice

## Ingredients
- Browned bits from cooked chicken
- Onion or garlic, chopped
- Water or stock
- Butter, cream, or oil
- Herbs, lemon juice, or sauce
- Salt
- Black pepper

## Steps
1. After cooking chicken, leave the browned bits in the pan.
2. Lower the heat to medium.
3. Add onion or garlic and stir for about 20 seconds.
4. Pour in water or stock.
5. Scrape the bottom of the pan to lift the browned bits.
6. Simmer for 1–2 minutes.
7. Turn off the heat and stir in butter, cream, or a little oil.
8. Finish with herbs, lemon juice, or sauce.
9. Add salt and black pepper to taste.

## Tips
1. The browned bits give the sauce most of its flavour.
2. Turn off the heat before adding butter or cream so the sauce stays smooth.
